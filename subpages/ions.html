Incredibly Iconic Ions!



Ah yes, ions! Beautiful things, but not as beautiful as my beloved atoms. It is of no difference, though. Ions are fundamental nonetheless, and on this page you will find a vast amount of incredibly important information about the incredibly iconic ions that exist in our world.

To put it simply, an ion is an atom that has been charged - be it positively or negatively - by the loss or gain of electrons. There are two main different types of ions; cations and anions.



Cations & Anions




Cations and Anions sound quite complicated, but I assure you, on my honour as Stephen Chakras, that they are in fact quite simple.



Cations are positive ions. In general, it is metals who will lose electrons to form a cation (remember, the more negative electrons that an atom loses, the more positive it becomes, as there will be more protons than electrons). The name of a cation (positive ion) is the same as the name of the element (i.e barium becomes barium ion).

Anions, as you may have guessed, are negatively charged atoms. It is typically non-metals which will gain electrons and make anions (there are more negative electrons than there are positive protons, so the charge is negative). The naming method of anions differ slightly, in order to be able to differentiate between cations and anions quickly. The name of an anion will be the stem name of the element with "-ide" added (i.e oxygen become oxide, meaning OX is the stem).
